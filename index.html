ABOUT

25 years of preserving gaming history

OpenBioForge is a free planned open-source recreation of the BioForge game engine, developed over 25 years to preserve Origin Systems' groundbreaking 1995 sci-fi adventure for modern platforms.

The project does not ship with the assets or content required to play BioForge. You must have a legally purchased copy of BioForge in order to run the game using this engine.

Enhanced graphics & physics

Modern rendering with realistic interactions. OpenBioForge replaces the original 1995 software renderer with OpenGL, eliminating pixelation and delivering crisp visuals. The project preserves BioForge's pioneering S.A.L.S.A. animation system while adding a custom physics engine for natural collisions and responsive interactions.

Responsive controls

Fluid movement that feels right. The hybrid locomotion system combines physics-based movement with selective root motion, addressing the original's notoriously clunky controls. Full gamepad support with analog controls provides the precise, modern input experience players expect.

Advanced modding capabilities

Revolutionary script transpilation technology. Original BioForge scripts are converted from their obfuscated format into readable C# using a sophisticated Roslyn-based transpiler. This breakthrough enables extensive modding, gameplay modifications, and entirely new experiences while maintaining perfect compatibility.

Cross-platform

Play anywhere, anytime. Built with minimal dependencies for maximum portability across Windows, Linux, and macOS. The lightweight architecture ensures consistent performance while keeping future platform expansions achievable.

Every aspect of the BioForge was designed with technology in mind. You are on a lonely planet because we could not show more than two to three characters on screen at a time. Your body has been operated on because the fledgling skeletal animation technology was too stiff to show smooth human animation.
— Ken Demarest, Director of BioForge

TRAILER

30th Anniversary Showcase

After 30 years, OpenBioForge brings the pioneering 1995 DOS game BioForge into the modern era. The original introduced the first fully texture-mapped 3D characters in gaming and influenced classics like Grim Fandango.

This work-in-progress preserves BioForge's revolutionary S.A.L.S.A. animation system and quaternion-based skeletal animation for future generations while adding enhanced graphics, responsive controls, and improved physics. The source code will be made available under GPL v3 once development is complete.
